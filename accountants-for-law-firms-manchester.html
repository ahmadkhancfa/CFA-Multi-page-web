SRA Accounts Rules

Understanding SRA Compliance

The SRA Accounts Rules exist to protect client money and ensure law firms maintain appropriate accounting records, controls and procedures. Compliance is not simply about producing year-end accounts. It requires firms to maintain accurate records throughout the year, perform regular reconciliations, identify breaches promptly and ensure client money is handled correctly.

For many firms, the challenge is not understanding the rules. The challenge is implementing practical systems and controls that ensure compliance on a day-to-day basis. At CFA Accountants, we support solicitors, legal practices and COFAs with the financial systems, reconciliations and reporting processes required to maintain compliance and reduce risk.

Three-Way Client Account Reconciliations

One of the most important controls under the SRA Accounts Rules is the three-way reconciliation. A three-way reconciliation compares:

  • ✓The client bank account balance
  • ✓The client cashbook balance
  • ✓The total of individual client ledger balances

All three figures should agree. Where differences exist, they must be investigated and corrected promptly. Three-way reconciliations help identify posting errors, missing transactions, incorrect transfers, unallocated receipts and client money shortages.

Failure to maintain accurate reconciliations can expose firms to regulatory breaches and increased compliance risk. The SRA Accounts Rules require firms to keep accounting records properly written up and reconciled to ensure client money remains protected.

Office Account vs Client Account

Many compliance issues arise because firms fail to distinguish correctly between office money and client money. Client money belongs to clients or third parties. Office money belongs to the firm. Incorrect transfers between these accounts can create serious compliance concerns.

We help firms establish robust procedures for:

✓Billing workflows
✓Client-to-office transfers
✓Residual balances
✓Matter closure procedures
✓Bank reconciliations
✓Ledger reviews

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a three-way reconciliation? +
A three-way reconciliation compares the client bank balance, client cashbook balance and total client ledger balances to ensure all records agree.
How often should three-way reconciliations be performed? +
The SRA requires firms holding client money to perform reconciliations at least every five weeks.
What happens if a reconciliation does not balance? +
Differences should be investigated immediately and supporting documentation retained. Unresolved differences must be reported as potential breaches.
What is client money? +
Client money is money held or received by a law firm relating to regulated legal services and belonging to a client or third party.
What is a residual balance? +
A residual balance is money remaining in a client account after a matter has concluded and all proper payments have been made.
