What the Service Is

Your CT600, Computed and Filed

Our Corporation Tax service manages the complete calculation, preparation and electronic submission of your company’s annual CT600 return. Every active UK limited company is legally required to compute its taxable profits and submit a formal tax declaration to HMRC alongside its statutory accounts.

We manage the entire process — analysing your business expenses, adjusting accounting profits for tax purposes, applying relevant capital allowances and filing your returns securely to maintain complete compliance.

Compliance

Bookkeeping for HMRC Corporate Compliance

Accurate Corporation Tax reporting depends on the structure of your daily bookkeeping. It is not simply a matter of totalling annual sales and costs; HMRC rules require clear tracking so tax-sensitive expenses are correctly categorised throughout the year.

Certain day-to-day costs are treated differently for tax — some cannot be offset against profits (such as client entertaining), while others qualify for specific reliefs. By keeping your records updated on a continuous cloud system, we ensure every transaction maintains an appropriate audit trail, capturing all legitimate allowable expenditure accurately and protecting you against late-filing or inaccuracy penalties.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

When is Corporation Tax actually due for payment to HMRC? +
While you have up to 12 months after your year-end to formally submit your CT600, the deadline to settle any tax owed is tighter: within 9 months and 1 day following the close of your accounting period. We track these overlapping timelines carefully to protect you from automated late fees.
What counts as allowable expenditure for a limited company? +
An expense is generally tax-deductible if incurred ‘wholly and exclusively’ for your business trade — staff salaries, raw materials, office rent, business insurance and specific professional fees. Expenses with dual personal and business use must be apportioned correctly, and items like client entertainment are legally excluded from tax relief.
